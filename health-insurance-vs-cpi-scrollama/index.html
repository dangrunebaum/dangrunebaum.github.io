The Cost of Health Insurance

We compared the cost of health insurance to inflation since 1999.

In 1999, Americans paid an average $2,196 a year for typical employer health insurance. By 2005, health insurance cost $4,024 a year, up over 80%. In comparison, inflation gained about 17%. Scroll down to track the cost of health insurance vs. inflation through 2021.

By 2010, health insurance cost $5,049 a year, up 130%. Inflation rose about 30%.

By 2015, health insurance cost Americans $6,251 a year, a gain of 185%. Inflation was up about 40%.

By 2021, health insurance cost $7,739 a year, a rise of over 250%. Inflation was up about 60%.

Looked at another way, in 1999 the average American worked 2 weeks to pay for health insurance. In 2021 that grew to about 6 weeks.

One further chart explains why the cost of health insurance has grown so much faster than inflation. See it below.

The rising cost of health insurance is almost entirely accounted for by the growing cost of Americans' healthcare itself. Between 1999 and 2020, the average cost of employer health insurance grew 240%. Over the same period, the cost of healthcare rose 224%.
