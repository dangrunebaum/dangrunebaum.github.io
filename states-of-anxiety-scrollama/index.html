Anxiety and the COVID-19 Vaccine

Which state saw anxiety ease the most?

The COVID-19 pandemic started in winter 2020, arriving in waves.

In April 2020, the CDC began to track the pandemic's mental health impact.

The Household Pulse Survey shows anxiety rising nationwide to a year-end peak before plunging in 2021.

The launch of COVID-19 vaccines saw a dramatic easing in the national anxiety rate.

The state where anxiety dropped the most in 2021 is Wyoming.

The number of residents reporting anxiety symptoms dropped by 52% from January to April.
